Challenge the Pavement Parking Ban

Scotland’s blanket pavement parking ban is affecting communities in ways the government didn’t anticipate. Many streets are now less safe, less accessible, and more stressful for everyday families — all while councils continue to block pavements with bins and fail to provide alternatives.

Why this matters:
Practical shared use of space is being criminalised in favour of one-size-fits-all enforcement. Streets that functioned safely for years are now seeing bin delays, blocked driveways, prams pushed into traffic, and rising neighbour conflict. Councils already had powers to act on genuinely obstructive parking — this new law punishes everyone for the actions of a few.

If the cost of enforcing this approach outweighs the benefit, the legislation must be changed or scrapped entirely.
